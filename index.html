What are attributes?

Attributes are things intrinsic to our nature, something Tom Wolfe famously called, "the right stuff." They are what allow a person to function at the highest level no matter how badly circumstances go sideways. They are those innate traits that determine how an individual will absorb, process and respond to the world around them. Attributes are not easily taught and they inform, rather than direct, behavior. While a skill might tell us what to do in a situation, attributes determine how we approach and handle that situation. They are difficult to assess, measure and test and can only be teased out in different situations and environments.

Skills are different. Skills are things that anyone can learn. Sure, skill competency and mastery can vary, but anyone can learn the fundamentals. In other words, skills direct behavior in specific situations and environments. They tell us how to behave to get certain things done. Skills also tend to be easy to assess, measure and test. They can be broken down into component parts and examined.

But what about in unknown situations? This is where your attributes come in. Attributes direct behavior in unknown, stressful and uncertain situations. It is these attributes that determine how successful both individuals and teams really are.
